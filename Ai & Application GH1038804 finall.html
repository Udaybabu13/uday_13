Dataset overview¶

The Dataset contains data scraped from cars24, a leading site for selling & purchasing second hand cars. It offers detailed information on high volume of second-hand cars including:¶

>Model name & manufacturing year
>Price & KM Driven (milage)
>Engine Capacity & Fuel Type
>Transmission Type (Manual/Automatic)
>Ownership History (First-hand, Second-hand, etc.)
>Availability of spare key
>Imperfections & Repainted parts

It contains 11 columns & 1,445 rows

Final Discussion & Conclusion¶

Strengths of the analysis¶

>The Study involved basic features such as pricing, fuel type, transmission & popularity of various car models, thereby enabling a detailed analysis of the used car market.
>Data-Driven Decision Making This study outlines trends on car pricing, fuel options & demand variability, thus helping the client to better inform pricing strategies.
>Market trends witnessed the diesel decline, rise of automatics & demand for affordable models are all reflective of changing consumer preferences.
>Competitive Intelligence knowing what car models & brands are leading in the market assists in the strategic inventory planning & focused marketing.

Limitations of the Study¶

>Dataset Size with 1,445 Rows, the dataset might not represent the full scope of the used car market but the decent scope of market.
>Missing Factors like External consideration such as location, Brand, reputation or seasonal fluctuations in demand were not  provided but mayinfluence pricing.
>The dataset focused on car specifications but lacks customer reviews or feedback that could provide additional insights into preferences.

Key Insights & Business Implications¶

>Demand & Inventory Management: Knowing the best-selling automobile models assists in stocking highly demanded cars.
>A brand with many best-selling models has high market share investing in these brands can increase sales.
>The firm can implement Ai-driven dynamic pricing to vary inventory based on shifting demand.

>Pricing strategy optimization:High price dominant place themselves in higher price segments,highlighting the prominence of high-end cars in upper market segments.
>The positively skewed price distribution demonstrates that affordability drives most purchases.
>A cost-plus pricing strategy will help ensure profitability while remaining competitive.

>Customer-centric growth: Offering certified pre-owned cars with warranties can boost consumer confidence.
>Price segment individualized financial plans can utilized to increase conversions.
>Automatics & petrol car can be marketed towards changing consumer preferences.

>Market Trends & Consumer preferences: Diesel vehicles are decreasing with a move towards petrol & potentially electric in the years to come.
>Automatics are pricier & in demand, indicating that buyers are placing a premium on convenience rather than cost savings.
>Older cars depreciate, yet some have high values since they are luxury or collector cars.

Recommendations for Cars24¶

>Optimize pricing structures based on age, fuel type, & mileage to maximize profits.
>Stock high-demand models & avoid low-selling models in order to reduce inventory costs.
>Invest in automatic & petrol vehicles since they are in higher demand compared to manual/diesel vehicles.
>Improve customer experience with warranties & financing solutions specific to price segments.
>Monitor shifting trends in electric/hybrid vehicles to be prepared for demand shifts in the future.
